2. BOSTON CELTICS

KEY ADDITIONS KEY LOSSES
Kristaps Porzingis Marcus Smart
Svy Mykhailiuk Grant Williams

PREVIOUS RECORD: 57-25 (2nd seed)

The Boston Celtics had a very strong season last year and were the favorites to make it out the eastern conference. However, the heroic run by Butler and the rest of the Heat stopped them short in the Eastern Conference Finals. Now, I know what Celtics fans are feeling right now, excited yet heartbroken. The trade for Kristaps Porzingis this offseason was a big deal, as Boston shipped away their beloved hometown hero in Marcus Smart. To Celtics fans, Smart represented the heart and soul of the team. He was willing to put his body on the line in order to win a game for his team, hustling for loose balls or exerting an extraordinary amount of energy on the defensive end in order to come up with a key stop. His leadership was huge and present among everyone, anchoring the strong defense that Boston had. The 2022 Defensive Player of the Year will now be playing for the Memphis Grizzlies and serve as a mentor for the young stars over there. Another loss for the Celtics is Grant, who gave them great minutes knocking down threes and sometimes guarding the opposing team's best player. Even though Boston lost Smart and Grant, they gained a 7'3'', 240 pound stretch big in Porzingis. He's coming off one of his best seasons were he produced a career high in ppg (23.2), steals (0.9), and field goal percentage (49.8%) as a member of the Washington Wizards. He will provide excellent floor spacing, shooting at 38.5% from three-point territory, and add onto the terrific front court defense that the Celtics already have with his long wingspan and ability to block shots. A front court of Robert Willams and KP will be a shot blocking party for any opponent trying to drive into the paint, not to mention that they have to get past Tatum and Brown on the wing, who are probably the leagues top defenders. Mykhailiuk will bring in his terrific three-point shooting (40.4% from deep last year) and take on a catch and shoot role. With Smart now gone, Derrick White and Malcolm Brogdon will get extended minutes in the back court, two very talented guards who can score at will, as well as contain their opponent defensively. With all this talent that Boston posesses, it's hard to not see them making it out of the eastern conference and compete for the Larry O'Brien trophy.

PROJECTED RECORD: 59-23 (1st seed)
